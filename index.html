About

The Idea of cyber relate straight to anything that have do with technology which of course in nowadays involve computers.

Braking it more simply, it's something to do with information/data in the computers world, such as IT (Information Technologies), Digital Communication, Information Security as so on ...

The familiar fields are:

Cybersecurity:

  • Protecting computer systems, networks & data from unauthorized access, cyberattacks & data breaches.
  • it's a constant race in developing strategies, tools & technologies to protect digital assets & mitigate threats.

Cybercrime Investigation:

  • Specializing in identifying, tracking & prosecuting individuals/groups involved in illegal activities through digital channels.
  • Using forensic techniques & digital evidence to uncover cybercrimes as hacking, identity theft & online fraud.

Network Security:

  • Designing, implementing & managing security to protect the networks from unauthorized access, data interception & network breaches.
  • Using firewalls, IDS (Intrusion Detection Systems) & many more solutions ensuring 2 of the fundamental idea in the information security world integrity & confidentiality of network communications.

Information Assurance:

  • Focusing on ensuring the confidentiality, integrity & availability of sensitive information in organizations (fundamental idea in the information security world).
  • Developing policies, procedures & controls to manage risks related to data security, privacy & compliance with regulations & standards.

Ethical Hacking:

  • Assessing security of computer systems & networks by identifying vulnerabilities & weaknesses that malicious hackers could exploit (Known as penetration testers/white-hat hackers).
  • Conducting authorized penetration tests & security assessments, helping organizations improve their cybersecurity defences.

Note: Of course that's just a small part of cyber-related disciplines, when each of them has a crucial part in todays modern society.

Firewall:

  • Firewall in cybersecurity refer to a security devices or software designed to monitor & control incoming & outgoing network traffic based on predetermined security rules.
  • Acting as a barrier between trusted internal networks & untrusted external networks.
  • Firewalls play a crucial role in preventing unauthorized access, detecting malicious activity & providing a line of defence against cyber threats.

What's methods:

  • Within the cyber realm, there are many methods that are fundamental to protecting systems, identifying vulnerabilities & responding to threats.
  • These methods are structured approaches that guide cybersecurity professionals in safeguarding digital assets & ensuring the integrity of information systems.

Some key methods in cybersecurity:

Risk Assessment:

  • Identifying, evaluating & prioritizing potential risks to an organization's assets, systems & data.
  • Helping organizations understand their threat landscape & allocate resources effectively to mitigate risks.

Penetration Testing:

  • A simulated cyberattack on a system, network or application to identify vulnerabilities that could be exploited by malicious actors.
  • Penetration testing helps organizations identify weaknesses & implement measures to strengthen their security posture.

Security Auditing:

  • A systematic evaluation of an organization's security policies, procedures & controls to ensure compliance with industry standards & best practices.
  • Helping organizations identify gaps in their security measures & improve their overall security framework.

Incident Response:

  • Structured approach to handling & managing security incidents, including detecting, investigating, mitigating & recovering from cyber incidents.
  • Effective incident response helps minimize the impact of security breaches & restore normal operations quickly.

Threat Modelling:

  • Analyzing a system or application to identify potential threats, vulnerabilities & attack vectors.
  • Helping organizations proactively design security measures to protect against potential threats.

Security Awareness Training:

  • Educating employees & stakeholders about cybersecurity best practices, potential threats & safe online behaviour.
  • Helping to create a security-conscious culture within the organization & reduce the risk of human error.

Data Encryption:

  • Transforming data into an unreadable format to protect it from unauthorized access.
  • Ensuring that sensitive information remains confidential & secure, both in transit & at rest.

Access Control:

  • Restricting access to systems, networks & data based on predefined security policies.
  • Ensuring that only authorized individuals have access to sensitive information & resources.

What's techniques:

  • Within the dynamic field of cybersecurity, various techniques are employed to detect, prevent & mitigate cyber threats.
  • These techniques leverage a combination of technological, procedural & human factors to enhance security & protect digital assets.

Key techniques in cybersecurity:

Intrusion Detection Systems (IDS):

  • Monitoring network traffic for suspicious activity & potential threats.
  • IDS solutions help identify & alert security teams to potential breaches or malicious activity.

Intrusion Prevention Systems (IPS):

  • Proactively blocking & preventing detected threats from compromising systems or networks.
  • IPS solutions provide an additional layer of defence by stopping malicious activity in real-time.

Security Information & Event Management (SIEM):

  • Aggregating & analyzing security data from multiple sources to provide comprehensive insights into potential threats.
  • SIEM solutions help organizations detect, investigate & respond to security incidents more effectively.

Endpoint Detection & Response (EDR):

  • Monitoring endpoints for suspicious behaviour & potential threats.
  • EDR solutions provide detailed visibility into endpoint activity & enable rapid response to security incidents.

Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA):

  • Adding an extra layer of security by requiring multiple forms of verification for access to systems or data.
  • MFA helps reduce the risk of unauthorized access by enhancing authentication processes.

Patch Management:

  • Regularly updating software & systems to fix vulnerabilities & improve security.
  • Patch management helps protect against known exploits & keeps systems secure.

Network Segmentation:

  • Dividing a network into smaller segments to limit the spread of potential threats.
  • Network segmentation enhances security by isolating critical systems & containing breaches.
